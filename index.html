It's a spectrum

There's no such thing as
"just picky."

Everyone's relationship with food is different — and it shifts. You might be adventurous at your favorite spot and a survival eater on vacation. Confident ordering alone but anxious in a group. These aren't categories. They're dimensions you move along constantly. See if any of these sound familiar.

01

The Preferences Eater

"No fatty meats, love spicy food, not big on cream sauces."

You'll eat most things. You just know what you like. You've probably said "I'm not picky, I just..." more times than you can count. You want the best dish on the menu for your palate, not just any safe option.

02

The Safe Foods Eater

"On vacation I always ask for plain rice or chicken nuggets."

You're not exploring — you're surviving. You've mastered the art of finding something edible anywhere, but it shouldn't have to feel like a mission. You just want to eat without the mental load.

03

The Texture Person

"It's not the taste, it's the... feeling? I can't explain it."

You've gagged on something everyone else loved and felt embarrassed about it. Slimy, mushy, gristly — you know exactly what you can't handle even if you struggle to put it into words. Menus never tell you what you actually need to know.

04

The Sensory Eater

"I just don't want any surprises on the plate."

Too many flavors competing, unexpected sweetness in a savory dish, a crunch where there shouldn't be one. It's not about being unadventurous — it's about knowing exactly what you're getting. You eat with your whole nervous system, not just your tongue.

05

The Cultural Comfort Eater

"I know what I grew up eating. Everything else is a gamble."

Your palate was shaped by a specific cuisine and it's deeply comfortable there. At an unfamiliar restaurant, "spicy" could mean anything from a mild kick to a hospital visit. You're not closed-minded — you just need a better translator.

06

The Expanding Eater

"I used to be way pickier. I'm trying to branch out."

You've been on a journey and you're proud of how far you've come. You want gentle nudges, not pressure. "This is 80% things you love, with one new ingredient" is exactly the kind of push that works for you.

07

The Social Anxiety Eater

"I'd rather eat something I don't love than ask for modifications."

You're not even that picky — but the thought of being "that person" at the table, asking the waiter to hold the sauce, requesting substitutions... you'd honestly rather just pick the safest-looking thing and deal with it.

Most people are a blend of several of these — and it changes depending on where you are, who you're with, and how your day went. Menu Buddy doesn't put you in a box. It learns your dimensions and adapts.

Core Features

Everything between you
and a good meal.

01

Smart Food Profile

No giant forms. An AI conversation builds your profile naturally — asking about textures, flavor combos, cooking methods, and intensity levels. "I don't love onions" vs "raw onion and I'm sending it back" are very different things.

Ingredient likes & dislikes with intensity
Texture, temperature & cooking preferences
Allergy vs preference distinction
Learns from your feedback over time
02

Menu Scanner

Snap a photo of any menu or paste text. Vision framework extracts the text, AI parses the dishes, and matches everything against your profile in seconds.

03

Hidden Ingredient Warnings

The killer feature. "Caesar salad" doesn't say anchovies — but they're in the dressing. Menu Buddy knows cuisine-specific secrets and flags what menus don't tell you.

04

Translate & Order

In Chile and need pizza with no cheese? Get the exact phrase in natural Spanish — culturally aware, not Google Translate robotic. Show the waiter your phone.

05

Transparent Reasoning

Never just "safe" or "avoid." Every recommendation shows its reasoning. "This looks good, but note that Thai curries often contain shrimp paste even when not listed."

How It Works

From menu to meal
in four steps.

1

Build Your Profile

Have a quick chat with the AI about what you like, hate, and are allergic to. Takes about 2 minutes. Syncs to iCloud.

2

Scan or Paste a Menu

Point your camera at a physical menu or paste text from a website. Vision framework extracts everything.

3

Get Instant Analysis

Every dish rated green, yellow, or red. Hidden ingredients flagged. Modification suggestions included.

4

Order With Confidence

Abroad? Generate a natural-sounding phrase in the local language. Show the waiter your phone if needed.

Design Philosophy
→

Show the reasoning

Never just "safe" or "avoid." Every recommendation explains why, so you make the final call.

→

Never claim certainty

"This likely contains X because Y" — not "this is definitely safe." It's someone else's kitchen.

→

No judgment

Whether you eat everything or only 12 foods, the app treats you the same. No "you should try..." pressure.

→

Err on the side of flagging

A false positive beats a false negative. Better to warn about something that's fine than miss something that isn't.
